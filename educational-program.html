↳EDUCATIONAL
PROGRAM:




Our public program is conformed by the Educational Program, PAOS’ most potent offer that thanks to its constance it has formed a captive audience. It is complemented with activities of different formats such as:

AIKIDO, FUNES Collaborative Documentation Center, The Displaced Artistic Practices seminary (PAD); The Future Series, a project about art, technology, and future; and #Cruce, a professional plan which allows us to strengthen our bonds with independent spaces and internationalization of the educational scheme.

↙PAD

Artistic Displaced Practices is an international seminary in six sessions for artists and multidisciplinary creators interested in acquiring tools to enrich and expand their work and research methodologies.

PAD happens in PAOS GDL in deferred sessions and their main goal is to detect the interdisciplinary displacement of artistic practices and offer, from a critique perspective, tools for the discussion and the development of artistic projects related to art production but also related to education, curatorial practices, writing and cultural management.

↙AIKIDO GDL

AIKIDO is a platform for dialogue and exchange between students of art, art history and related degrees, self-taught and emerging creators. AIKIDO consists of sessions designed as a critical accompaniment where researchers and young artists have the opportunity to draw a series of collective responses to exercises and questions that allow them to analyze the singularities of their context, history and production.

Within the sessions, more experienced guest agents shared brief anecdotes that helped to nourish and motivate the workshop participants, always emphasizing the human part of our practice, that is, the errors, disappointments, efforts involved, expectations and difficult moments. It is important to emphasize that it is not about art classes where the attendees obtain the knowledge given by those who taught and managed the project.

The idea is that every person who participates in the program is involved in an honest and vulnerable way in the pursuit of ideas, ethics, models and passions that allow us to produce new enthusiasm within our professional field.

AIKIDO is a project of Lugar Común, Monterrey. Now it is also carried out in Guadalajara through PAOS GDL and in Puebla through the Museo Amparo.

AIKIDO GDL, first season, was guided by Paulina Ascencio, in collaboration with the artist Claudia Cisneros and Lorena Peña Brito.

↙THE FUTURE SERIES

The Future Series is an interview project that addresses the theme of the future from multiple perspectives, led by the designer and manager Aditi Ruiz (PAOS student). The intention is to share knowledge in a transversal manner, taking an inclusive and critical stance on the human condition and our relationship with culture and technology. Talk about the future forces us to question the present and explore reality from new approaches. The objective is not to predict but to speculate, and that these conversations allow us to visualize new parameters of what is possible. Aditi Ruiz (Mexico, 1987) is a designer and researcher interested in contemporary utopias. Her design philosophy is based on a critical approach and the exploration of fictitious scenarios. Her work focuses on social phenomena, conceptual experiments and induce reflection through design.
